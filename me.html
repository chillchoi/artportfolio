What I do

I turn complicated things — trials, markets, cross-border rules, whole industries — into something people can actually act on.

I study Public Health and Data Science at UC Berkeley, graduating in 2027. My path has not been a straight line: I started making digital art about chemistry, served as a Sergeant in the Korean Air Force, and worked across healthcare strategy, cross-border law, and AI product. What ties it together is one instinct — take something invisible or overwhelming, and make it clear enough to move on.

That is why I am drawn to healthcare and life sciences. Every commercial or operational decision there can ultimately reach a clinician or a patient. Strategy is what decides whether good technology actually gets there. I like being the person who helps it arrive.

I work best when given ownership. Give me a hard, ambiguous problem and I will be diligent, resourceful, and genuinely happy doing it.
