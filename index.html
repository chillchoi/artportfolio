I am Justin Joonseo Choi, an interactive artist and Berkeley student who never stopped drawing. This is a body of work about the invisible, meaning chemistry, energy, and the environment, translated into color and composition so that ideas too small or too abstract to see can finally be felt. Most of it began as a way to make science make sense to me, and it slowly became the through-line of everything else I do.

Art has always been the thread running through everything else.

My name is Justin Joonseo Choi. I am a Public Health and Data Science student at UC Berkeley, somewhere between finishing my service in the Korean Air Force and figuring out what comes next. I have been told that is a strange combination of things, and I agree.

Before consulting or market strategy, I was making digital illustrations about chemistry, trying to explain ideas like electron uncertainty through color and composition. That instinct to translate the invisible into something felt is still what drives most of the work here. I do not know exactly what kind of artist this makes me, but I have learned to stop worrying about that.
